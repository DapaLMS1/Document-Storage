Why Keep Records?

The long-term storage of records is essential for responding to future audits, legal inquiries, or post-operative infection investigations. Click the topics below to learn more about the specific requirements.

Under Australian regulatory requirements, all reprocessing records must be maintained in a secure, designated storage area for a minimum of seven years.
Most sterilisers produce thermal paper printouts which fade significantly over time. To ensure these remain legible for the full seven-year period, printouts must be scanned or photocopied onto standard paper.

Every steriliser must undergo formal validation every 12 months (or after major repairs). This involves two key components:

  • Performance Qualification (PQ): An authorized technician tests the machine using your practice's actual instrument loads to confirm steam reaches the center of your specific kits and loading patterns.
  • Calibration: Ensuring the internal sensors (the "brain" of the machine) accurately reflect the temperature and pressure inside the chamber.
